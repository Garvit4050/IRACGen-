4. Exigent circumstances may allow for a warrantless search, as long as the circumstances necessitate immediate action to prevent harm or the destruction of evidence.

5. Terry v. Ohio (1968) established that a police officer may conduct a limited search, or "frisk," of a suspect's outer clothing for weapons if the officer has a reasonable suspicion that the suspect is armed and dangerous.

6. Miranda v. Arizona (1966) established that before any custodial interrogation, law enforcement officers must inform the suspect of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney, to ensure that any statements made are voluntary and not coerced.

Exceptions and limitations to these rules may apply depending on the specific circumstances of each case. It is essential to consider the unique facts and legal context when determining the admissibility of evidence obtained through a search and seizure.
